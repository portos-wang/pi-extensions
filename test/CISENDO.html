📋 Executive Summary

The CISENDO® test is a non-invasive DNA methylation diagnostic assay developed by CISPOLY (Beijing, China) for the early detection of endometrial cancer (EC). The test detects hypermethylation of two tumor suppressor genes—CDO1 (Cysteine Dioxygenase Type 1) and CELF4 (CUGBP Elav-like Family Member 4)—in cervical brushing cells using real-time PCR technology.

🧬 About the CISENDO® Test

Technology Overview

The CISENDO® DNA Methylation Detection Kit utilizes real-time polymerase chain reaction (PCR) to quantify methylation levels of CDO1 and CELF4 genes in cervical exfoliated cells. The test measures the difference in cycle threshold (ΔCt) values between target genes and the internal control gene (GAPDH).

Test Parameters

Target Genes: CDO1 (Cysteine Dioxygenase Type 1) & CELF4 (CUGBP Elav-like Family Member 4)
Sample Type: Cervical brushing cells (non-invasive)
Detection Method: Real-time PCR
Positive Result Criteria: ΔCtC1 ≤ 8.4 (CDO1) OR ΔCtC4 ≤ 8.8 (CELF4)
Internal Control: GAPDH (Glyceraldehyde-3-phosphate dehydrogenase)
Regulatory Status: NMPA Class III (No. 20243402610) | CE-IVD

Biological Rationale

Hypermethylation of CpG islands in promoter regions of tumor suppressor genes leads to gene silencing, a hallmark of cancer development. CDO1 and CELF4 are frequently hypermethylated in endometrial cancer tissues and can be detected in cervical exfoliated cells shed from the endometrium, enabling non-invasive detection.

💡 Clinical Recommendations

Recommended Clinical Applications

  1. Triage Tool: Use CISENDO® to triage women with abnormal uterine bleeding (AUB) or postmenopausal bleeding (PMB) before invasive procedures
  2. Risk Stratification: Identify high-risk women who need hysteroscopy/biopsy vs. those who can be safely monitored

Step 1: Patient presents with AUB/PMB → Perform TVS

Step 2: If ET elevated OR clinical suspicion → Perform CISENDO® test

Step 3: If CISENDO® positive → Refer for hysteroscopy/biopsy

Step 4: If CISENDO® negative with high clinical suspicion → Consider repeat testing or direct biopsy

Final Verdict

The CISENDO® test represents a significant advancement in non-invasive endometrial cancer screening. With demonstrated high diagnostic accuracy, NMPA and CE-IVD approvals, and equivalence to invasive sampling methods, it offers a valuable triage tool for clinicians managing women with suspected endometrial pathology. The test is particularly valuable for detecting EC in women with normal endometrial thickness, a limitation of traditional ultrasound-based screening. While further validation in diverse populations and regulatory approvals in additional markets (e.g., FDA) would enhance its global impact, the current evidence strongly supports its clinical utility.
